In a 1994 book, German scholar Echard Sander argued that the Snow White tale is based on a real person, Countess Margarete von Waldeck of Bavaria.
The countess was raised in an area where children working in copper mines were so stunted that they were called dwarfs.

The Grimm fairy tale gets a Technicolor treatment in Disney's first animated feature.
Jealous of Snow White's beauty, the wicked queen orders the murder of her innocent stepdaughter,
but later discovers that Snow White is still alive and hiding in a cottage with seven friendly little miners.
Disguising herself as a hag, the queen brings a poisoned apple to Snow White, who falls into a death-like sleep that can be broken only by a kiss from the prince.
